# Gregory Castellanos Website Refinement Audit

## Current Direction

The site is now a simpler personal umbrella site for Greg Castellanos. It introduces Greg, explains six connected areas of work, links to venture destinations where available, shows selected proof, and gives visitors a straightforward way to start a conversation.

## What Was Adjusted

- Reduced public navigation to Home, Ventures, About, and Contact.
- Consolidated the old People, Ideas, Brands, Technology, Experiences, Places, Work, and Speaking routes into canonical pages with lightweight aliases.
- Restored the approved homepage hero copy.
- Reworked the six areas as an editorial numbered venture index.
- Created a Ventures directory with Current Practices and Selected Ventures And Projects.
- Kept Contact text-led, with the form near the top and no oversized portrait.
- Added light and dark themes with a manual toggle, OS preference fallback, and localStorage persistence.
- Kept private details off the public site while using the public work email from the source bank.

## Professional Design Notes

- The current logo has the right emotional territory: sun, landscape, path, navy, and gold. Next production steps are transparent-background exports, responsive lockups, and a simplified small-space mark.
- The site should avoid adding more public lanes. The six areas are enough.
- The generated editorial image works as temporary atmosphere, but real teaching photos, events, tools, selected project images, and place images will make the site feel much more credible.
- The homepage should keep prioritizing clarity over autobiography. Visitors need to understand the shape of the work quickly.
- Work examples should become the next major content upgrade. Three to five approved case studies will do more than another layer of service copy.

## Recommended Next Assets

- Transparent logo files: horizontal, stacked, and icon-only.
- A current environmental portrait, not just a cutout headshot.
- Three to five real project photos with permission boundaries.
- Speaking or teaching photos.
- Event and place photos for Recreation Experiences and Make Places Better.
- Patent numbers or links for the audio technology.
- A small set of public case studies with approved privacy boundaries.
- LinkedIn URL and any approved public social links.

## Responsive Audit

Overflow checks were captured at 320, 375, 390, 768, 1024, 1440, and 1920 pixels for Home, Ventures, About, Contact, and Privacy. No horizontal overflow above 1px was detected in the automated pass.
