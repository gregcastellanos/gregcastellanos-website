# Gregory Castellanos Website Design Audit

## Current Direction

The site is strongest when it presents Gregory as a systems-minded multidisciplinary founder, educator, coach, producer, technologist, and creative operator. The clearest story is not "many services." It is "one person who helps people, projects, events, tools, and places become more coherent and usable."

## What Was Adjusted

- Added the temporary Gregory Castellanos logo to the header and social preview metadata.
- Preserved the six-area structure while simplifying the language inside each lane.
- Renamed the place-based venture to Castellanos Makes Places Better.
- Reduced resume-like density on the homepage and About page.
- Brought in verified source-bank signals: learning design, executive function coaching, AI evaluation and literacy, patented audio technology, Local Maker Mart, live production, and community programs.
- Kept private phone details off the public site while using the public work email from the source bank.

## Professional Design Notes

- The current logo has the right emotional territory: sun, landscape, path, navy, and gold. Next production steps are transparent-background exports, responsive lockups, and a simplified small-space mark.
- The current site should avoid adding more lanes. The six areas are enough. More detail belongs in selected case studies.
- The generated editorial images work as temporary atmosphere, but real portraits, teaching photos, events, tools, and place images will make the site feel much more credible.
- The homepage should keep prioritizing clarity over autobiography. Visitors need to understand the shape of the work quickly.
- Work examples should become the next major design upgrade. Three to five real case studies will do more than another layer of service copy.

## Recommended Next Assets

- Transparent logo files: horizontal, stacked, and icon-only.
- A current portrait.
- Three to five real project photos.
- Patent numbers or links for the audio technology.
- A small set of public case studies with approved privacy boundaries.
- LinkedIn URL and any approved public social links.
