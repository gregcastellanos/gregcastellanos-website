# Gregory Castellanos Website Design Audit

## Current Direction

The site is strongest when it presents Greg as a systems-minded multidisciplinary founder, educator, coach, producer, technologist, and creative operator. The clearest story is not "many services." It is "one person who helps people, projects, events, tools, and places become more coherent and usable."

## What Was Adjusted

- Preserved the temporary Gregory Castellanos logo while matching light and dark versions to the site backgrounds.
- Tightened the design system around navy, cream, gold, and natural green, with stronger responsive spacing and type rules.
- Kept the six-area structure and restored the place-based venture name to Common Ground Works.
- Reduced resume-like density on Home, About, Contact, Places, Speaking, and Work.
- Removed the large Contact portrait, moved the form higher, and made the page more direct.
- Reworked Work into an editorial portfolio index instead of a database-style listing.
- Kept private details off the public site while using the public work email from the source bank.

## Professional Design Notes

- The current logo has the right emotional territory: sun, landscape, path, navy, and gold. Next production steps are transparent-background exports, responsive lockups, and a simplified small-space mark.
- The current site should avoid adding more lanes. The six areas are enough. More detail belongs in selected case studies.
- The generated editorial images work as temporary atmosphere, but real teaching photos, events, tools, selected project images, and place images will make the site feel much more credible.
- The homepage should keep prioritizing clarity over autobiography. Visitors need to understand the shape of the work quickly.
- Work examples should become the next major content upgrade. Three to five approved case studies will do more than another layer of service copy.

## Recommended Next Assets

- Transparent logo files: horizontal, stacked, and icon-only.
- A current environmental portrait, not just a cutout headshot.
- Three to five real project photos with permission boundaries.
- Speaking or teaching photos.
- Event and place photos for Recreation Experiences and Common Ground Works.
- Patent numbers or links for the audio technology.
- A small set of public case studies with approved privacy boundaries.
- LinkedIn URL and any approved public social links.

## Responsive Audit

Screenshots and overflow checks were captured at 320, 375, 390, 768, 1024, 1440, and 1920 pixels for Home, About, Contact, Places, Speaking, and Work. No horizontal overflow above 1px was detected in the automated pass.
